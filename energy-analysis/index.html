Step 03

Why are regions different?

Finding
Structure explains stability.
TAS: dispatchable hydro — 99% renewable, low volatility
SA: variable wind/solar + gas backup — 71% renewable, high volatility
03
Term
Dispatchability — the ability of a generation source to be turned on, ramped up, or throttled down on demand, independent of weather or time of day. Hydro is fully dispatchable; wind and solar are not. This distinction — not total renewable volume — explains why TAS and SA behave so differently under the same grid stress.
